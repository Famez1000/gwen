Gwyn is designed to help you manage your anxiety. In the app, you'll find practical tools and techniques to help you cope with, understand, and gradually heal your anxiety.

The app combines breathing exercises, grounding techniques, journaling, mood check-ins, progress tracking, calming reflections, and supportive AI-powered guidance.

Gwyn helps you break the cycle of negative thinking and gradually replace it with a more positive, healthier mindset, reducing anxious feelings over time.

Features include:

• Breathing exercises
Use calming breathing patterns to slow your breathing, relax your body, and create space between yourself and anxious thoughts.

• Journaling and reflection
Write about your day, track your anxiety level, and let Gwyn summarize your journal entries into kind, helpful reflections.

• Mood and anxiety tracking
Record how you feel, identify patterns over time, and follow your progress through simple, supportive insights.

• Grounding tools
Use sensory grounding exercises to reconnect with the present moment when anxiety feels overwhelming.

• Reminders
Set gentle reminders to check in with yourself, breathe, reflect, or simply take a calming moment.

• Progress insights
Review your breathing sessions, calming streaks, anxiety logs, and reflections to discover what helps you most.

• Calming activities
Explore supportive exercises, affirmations, gentle distractions, and soothing activities whenever your mind needs a reset.

• Chat with Gwyn
Talk with Gwyn whenever anxiety feels overwhelming. Gwyn offers warm, practical support, grounding suggestions, and gentle encouragement.

Gwyn is designed to feel warm, simple, and human. It doesn't tell you to "just calm down." Instead, it guides you through small, manageable steps: breathe, notice, write, ground yourself, reflect, and keep moving forward.

Important: Gwyn is not a medical device, therapist, crisis service, or a replacement for professional medical or mental health care. If you are in immediate danger, think you may harm yourself or someone else, or need urgent help, contact your local emergency services or a trusted person immediately.

Think of Gwyn as your pocket companion—always there during anxious moments, quiet reflection, and everyday emotional support.
